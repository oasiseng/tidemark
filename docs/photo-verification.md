# Photo Verification Guide

## Why Photo Documentation Matters

Before concrete placement, the contractor must provide photo documentation of top-of-form elevations for engineer verification. This is a requirement of the FERE general notes and serves as the final check before an irreversible pour.

Good photos save phone calls. Bad photos delay pours.

---

## Required Photos (Minimum)

### 1. TideMark Stake Overview

**What to capture:** Full TideMark stake in context, showing:
- Stake location relative to benchmark
- Collar position on the graduated scale
- Stringline connected to the stake

**How to shoot:**
- Stand 6–10 feet away
- Landscape orientation
- Include ground/benchmark in frame
- Collar mark should be readable in the photo

### 2. Collar Close-Up

**What to capture:** Close-up of the collar position on the graduated scale.

**How to shoot:**
- 2–3 feet away
- Eye level with the collar
- Graduation marks clearly visible
- Number at collar position readable

### 3. Tape Measurement Verification

**What to capture:** Tape measure showing distance from benchmark to top of form.

**How to shoot:**
- Hold tape from benchmark reference point to top of form
- Both ends of measurement visible in frame
- Number on tape readable
- Include enough context to identify what you're measuring from and to

### 4. Formwork Overview

**What to capture:** Overall view of the formwork setup showing:
- Stringline in relation to top of forms
- General alignment and condition
- TideMark stake(s) visible in background

**How to shoot:**
- Stand back far enough to see the full form layout
- Stringline should be visible against the sky or a contrasting background

---

## Photo Quality Checklist

Before sending photos to the engineer, verify:

- [ ] Photos are in focus (not blurry)
- [ ] Numbers on tape measure / TideMark graduations are legible
- [ ] Each benchmark used has at least one stake photo
- [ ] Measurement photo shows both reference point and form top
- [ ] Timestamp on photos is current (don't send old photos)
- [ ] Photos are taken in daylight or with adequate lighting

---

## How to Send

Send photos to the engineer via:
- Email (preferred — creates a record)
- Text message (acceptable for quick review)
- Project management platform (if applicable)

Include in your message:
- Project address
- Date of photos
- Which benchmark(s) you measured from
- Your measured elevation offset (in inches)

**Example message:**
> "1234 John Rd — photos from today 3/15/2026. Set forms using Screw B as reference. Collar at +3 inches per exhibit. Tape measurement from screw head to top of form confirms 3 inches. Ready for verification before pour scheduled for 3/17."

---

## What the Engineer Is Looking For

When reviewing your photos, the engineer will verify:

1. **Correct benchmark used** — photos match the benchmark shown in the exhibit
2. **Correct offset** — collar position and tape measurement match the specified offset from the Elevation Offset Table
3. **Consistent measurements** — if multiple benchmarks are used, all offsets are internally consistent
4. **Form condition** — forms appear level, properly braced, and at the correct elevation
5. **No site changes** — ground conditions appear consistent with the exhibit (no unexpected grading or disturbance)

If anything looks off, the engineer will call before clearing the pour.
